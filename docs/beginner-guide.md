# Building Your AI — a beginner's guide

New to AI and not sure how to make it *yours*? This guide covers the ideas behind
the builder. You don't have to read it to use the tool — but it helps to know
what each choice is really doing.

## Why bother setting this up?

Out of the box, most AI assistants are agreeable, wordy, and a little generic.
That's fine for a quick question. But if you use one regularly, a few minutes of
setup changes it from a bland chat box into something that actually fits how you
think and work — a secretary, a companion, a hype builder, a devil's advocate,
whatever you need.

## The three things you're really deciding

1. **Who it is** — its role and default personality. Your go-to assistant.
2. **How it talks to you** — tone, length, bluntness, how much it pushes back.
3. **What it does on its own** — whether it waits for you, suggests next steps,
   or drives the work like a project manager.

The builder also handles a fourth, quieter one: **what it's allowed to remember**,
which matters more than most people realize.

## Picking a starting archetype

Don't overthink step 1. The archetypes are just sensible presets so you're not
staring at a blank form. Pick the one closest to what you want and adjust from
there. "Build My Own" starts everything in the middle.

| Archetype | Good when you want… |
|---|---|
| The Organizer | Planning, tracking, staying on top of tasks. |
| The Companion | To think out loud with a warm, patient presence. |
| The Motivator | Momentum, encouragement, and clear next steps. |
| The Challenger | Your ideas stress-tested before the real world does it. |
| The Teacher | Something explained at your level, step by step. |
| The Creator | A creative partner to riff and build with. |
| The Expert Assistant | Fast, confident, to-the-point answers. |

## The sliders: words, not numbers

Each slider becomes a written instruction in your profile — never a number. Slide
"Gentle ↔ Blunt" toward blunt and the profile says *"Be direct and blunt; don't
cushion the message,"* which an AI understands far better than `bluntness: 80%`.

The middle position of any slider means **"adapt to the moment"** rather than
locking in one behavior.

## Memory and privacy (read this one)

The defaults are deliberately privacy-forward:

- **May remember** — stable, low-risk things like your tools, goals, and projects.
- **Ask first** — anything personal: family, health, finances, other people.
- **Never store** — passwords, confidential records, and identifying information
  about students, clients, patients, or employees.

If you're a teacher, clinician, or anyone handling other people's data, keep the
"never store" tier switched on. It's the cheapest insurance in the whole tool.

## Testing and revising

A profile isn't done when you export it — it's done after you've used it a bit.
When a response feels off, name the defect and adjust:

- *Too wordy* → slide toward Concise, or export again after nudging "Shorter."
- *Too agreeable* → slide toward Challenging, turn on "Avoid excessive praise."
- *Too cold* → slide toward Casual/Playful, or nudge "Less robotic."
- *Asks too many questions* → turn on "Make low-risk assumptions automatically."
- *Not enough pushback* → turn on "Challenge weak assumptions."

Come back, tweak a slider or two, re-export, paste it in. Two or three rounds and
it'll feel like it was built for you — because it was.

## Where to paste your profile

- **ChatGPT** — Settings → Personalization → Custom Instructions (two boxes).
- **Claude** — Settings → Profile, or a Project's custom instructions.
- **Gemini** — Saved info / Gems.
- **Copilot** — the system/instructions field.
- **Anything else** — use the generic system prompt or the full Markdown profile.
